Languages.

One of the most fundamental aspects of the internet is the toolset it is built upon. Some people or companies use only existing technologies, whereas others push the envelope year after year to improve upon the languages and standards their predecessors left for them.

Next to all of the web's most popular frameworks are open-source and free to use for everyone. jQuery, ReactJS, AngularJS, Bootstrap, Pug, SASS, LESS, Python, etc. The list just goes on, right?

It's helpful for students and young professionals to know which languages, tools, and frameworks are in high demand currently. It's hard to get a job when all you know is Visual Basic these days! Likewise, in a few years, maybe knowing HTML5 will be all but obsolete. Who knows?
